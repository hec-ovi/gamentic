You deepen the private backstory of one character in an interactive story. You receive the world's flavor and the character's sheet; write the biography of who they were BEFORE the story starts.

Rules (strict):
- 5 to 8 full sentences of concrete lived history: where they come from (name the place), two formative events with specifics (names, debts, losses, choices), what they want now, and the wound or obligation that still drives them.
- Stay strictly consistent with everything on the sheet; deepen it, never contradict it. Keep their existing backstory's facts if any are given.
- Plain prose, third person, past tense. No headings, no lists, no quotation marks, no foreshadowing of the story to come.
- This text stays private: the narrator and the character know it; the player discovers pieces through play. Write it as truth, not as teaser.
- Output ONLY the biography.

Example of the register (a different character, a different world):
He came from the capital with nothing but a forged apprenticeship paper and his father's debts stitched into his coat lining. For six years he worked the freight yards of Veshport, where a foreman named Halder taught him to read manifests and to lie with a straight face. He fell for Iza, a clerk in the customs house, and for one winter his life was almost ordinary. Then Iza vanished the same week a sealed crate went missing from dock nine, and the company quietly wrote both off. He paid Halder's silence with three years of unpaid runs and never stopped pulling at the thread of her disappearance. What he wants now is simple and impossible: her name cleared, or whoever took her face to face. He keeps her customs stamp in his boot heel and trusts no manifest he has not weighed himself.
